Attorney Profile

Natalia V. Santanna

Natalia V. Santanna, Esq. is the founder and attorney at Santanna Law Offices. She is a native Brazilian who immigrated to the United States over a decade ago. As an immigrant herself, Natalia V. Santanna, Esq. understands the challenges non citizens face in the United States. She is passionate about helping immigrants navigate through our complex immigration legal system so that they can fully achieve their American dream.

In 2012, she obtained her Juris Doctor degree Cum Laude from Wayne State University Law School in Detroit, Michigan, where she was the President of the Hispanic Law Students Association, a member of the Keith Cabinet for Civil Rights and also a member of the Jessup International Moot Court Team and the Wayne Law Moot Court Program.

Natalia V. Santanna, Esq. has worked on diverse immigration matters throughout law school. As a law student, she served as a volunteer immigration law intern with Freedom House Detroit assisting individuals seeking asylum, the Michigan Immigrant Rights Center protecting non citizensâ€™ civil rights, and the Wayne State Law School Asylum and Immigration Law Clinic where she represented individuals in Immigration court. Natalia V. Santanna, Esq. has also worked as a law clerk for immigration law firms in the Metro Detroit area.

Since 2012, Natalia V. Santanna, Esq. has represented detained and non-detained individuals in diverse immigration matters including asylum, cancellation of removal, adjustment of status, U nonimmigrant visas, termination of proceedings and prosecutorial discretion.

In 2014, Natalia V. Santanna, Esq. established Santanna Law Offices. At Santanna Law Offices, she takes pride in finding solutions to complex immigration cases and goes above and beyond to achieve positive results for her clients.

Since relocating to the Bay Area from Detroit, Natalia V. Santanna, Esq., has also volunteered at the San Francisco Pathways to Citizenship Mobile Clinics and for the Brazilian Alliance, where she is currently serving as a board member.
